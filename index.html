Let’s make one of Spotify’s most popular features more user friendly.

#SpotifyPlaylistUX

Case Study

Empathize & Define

Design Question: How can we make the Spotify playlist section more organized and easier to navigate in the app?

Spotify is currently one of the most popular music streaming services, coming in at about 365 million active monthly users. The playlist feature is one of the most popular on the platform. Out of the 20% most followed playlists on Spotify, 50% are personalized. Music fosters incredible connection and users are tuned in.

As an avid Spotify user myself, and a creator and listener of playlists, I’ve noticed the “My Library” section that houses a user’s playlists is not specialized in its organization. It includes playlists, artists, albums, podcasts & shows, and downloaded content all in the same place.

Given the nature of the platform, and how popular Spotify playlists have become, I find it inefficient and inconvenient to the usability and organization of such a popular feature.

I believe users should have the ability to navigate to a separate section in their “My Library” just for playlists. Even further, I believe sectioning out playlists between ones a user has created and ones a user has saved is most efficient.

The social aspect of the platform is one of the major value ads and unique aspects of Spotify, being able to follow your friends and people whose music taste you share similarities with. Having a separate section for this defining feature makes the most sense given user behavior.
